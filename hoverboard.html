Hoverboard Redesign

*HCI Course Project

Overview

We evaluated the Hoverboard and redesigned it using low-fidelity prototypes to improve its safety, effectiveness and feedback.

My Role

Meeting facilitation, graphic design, product evaluation, presentation

Achievement

We were voted first place in the class competition 😆

Product Evaluation

The Hoverboard was a big hit in the market when it first came out. Probably because people tend to see that expensive things are quality too regarding to Vablen Effect. Regardless of its beautiful appearance, my team focused on its weaknesses, for which we came up with specific improvement recommendations in the next section.

Recommendations

Improvement 1 - Safety

We replace the 2 big wheels with 6 small wheels and the triangle structure will make it more stable. Also, we improved the sensor and made it move only when it senses two feet.

Improvement 2 - Portability

We implemented 2 solar panels on the board for more portable charging.

Improvement 3 - Accessibility, Feedback, Constraints

We added a mobile app that offers battery status, manual control mode, and GPS location.

Final Thoughts

This was my first product redesign experience with a team and it was satisfying. With our implementation of the wheels, the solar panels, and the app, users will obtain a safer user experience with more utilities. However, I saw our limitations. We redesigned based on purely theoretical perspectives, hence budgets, materials, and supporting techniques were not taken into consideration. I hope one day I will be able to build products from low-fidelity prototypes to high-fidelity prototypes, and eventually to an actual product being used.
