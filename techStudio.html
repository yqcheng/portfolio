COSI: Tech Studio Website

*Internship Project

Overview

As a Web Development Intern in COSI, I developed the Tech Studio department's website. Just like the Volunteer & Community's old website, the Tech Studio site was stacked with plain text.


My focus on this development was to improve the content architecture, aesthetics, hierarchy, and discoverability, as well as to reduce the signal-to-noise ratio.

My Role

Prototype, front-end development, usability testing, Joomla (Content Management System)

Ideation & Prototype

I started with organizing the content and divided it to different categories. Since the topics throughout the site were very distinctive: introduction, offers, workshops, and program reservation, but they were not displayed in a visible way. The first idea came to mind was to separate them by color bands, and adopt modularity in each section to improve the content discoverability. Below are my first wireframe and first mockup:

The head of Tech Studio requested to use the color scheme in their advertisement booklet. After several rounds of refinements, I replaced the incompatible brown color with a more visually pleasant background while using a specific color as requested, and increased the discoverability of some important information such as the hours of Tech Studio.

To increase the reliability of the program, we decided to used authority endorsement by adding bios of the team members.

Challenges

In the old site, "Adult Volunteer" and "Youth Volunteer" were mixed in the same page, but they have different processes of application and different positions. Therefore, the best way is to separate these two programs.

Final Thoughts

It felt good to develop and deploy my first website in such an important organization! I am genuinely glad to contribute to a program that dedicates itself to children's education. An important lesson I have learned in an organizational setting is that designs always have tradeoffs and as a designer I must balance the needs of different clients - and those needs are not always compatible.
