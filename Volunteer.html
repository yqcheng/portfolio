COSI: Volunteer Webpage

*Internship Project

Overview

I evaluated and redesigned the website for COSI's Volunteer and Community website to increase visibility of key function and optimize its information architecture and aesthetics

(before) (after)

My Role

Prototype, graphic design, usability testing

Website Evaluation

Think about this: you are a high school student looking for a volunteer position to fill in your service hours. You found COSI because it is a famous non-profit museum. You go online and search for the volunteer program. Now you are in the page, what next?

< My first impression of this page was: too much text!!

< This is a notice for the unavailability of the youth volunteer position. It is not visible, nor effective

< The placement of the buttons is very disorganized. It intended to use modulations to hide information, but those buttons are just so out of place

Organization

I first read through the site with a goal to declutter it and re-sort information. The content can be categorized as:

Then I proposed to 'fold' the information under its respect title instead of using buttons. In this way, the front page is more intuitive for users to navigate around.

In the old site, "Adult Volunteer" and "Youth Volunteer" were mixed in the same page, but they have different processes of application and different positions. Therefore, the best way is to separate these two programs.

Aesthetics & Discoverability

The whole page was stacked with text without any pictures. Users would be bored by the lengthy content while for a site as informative as this, any information is important. Here I added some pictures and color to bright up the page and increase content discoverbility.

Final Thoughts

During this process, there were some tradeoffs I needed to make. Because the main site has its own layout, fonts, and color scheme, I had to be consistent with the style, aka there was no fancy scrolling or change of navigation. A limitation for me was that I wasn't involved in the coding process, so the final website turned out differently from my design. To have more control over the substantiation of my design, I self-studied HTML & CSS and became a Web Development Intern later.
